AKAN NAMES

Akan naming is a tradition that originates from the Akan people of Ghana.
It's a culture where children are named after the day of week they were born and their gender.
The convention is widely shared in parts of Africa, with several notable figures spotting the "day names".
Ghana's president Kwame Nkurumah and former United Nations Secretary-General Kofi Annan are
examples of public figures with Akan names.
